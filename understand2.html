1. Bias

Bias can lead to unfair or inaccurate results and is a critical issue to address in AI development.

2. Artificial General Intelligence

AGI remains theoretical and is the goal of many AI researchers, aiming for machines that can perform any intellectual task that a human can do.

3. Training Data

This data set includes input-output pairs that the algorithm uses to learn how to map inputs to outputs.

4. Model

The model is used to make predictions or decisions without being explicitly programmed to perform the task.

5. Overfitting

Overfitting occurs when a model learns the detail and noise in the training data to the extent that it negatively impacts the performance of the model on new data.

6. Reinforcement Learning

The agent learns from the consequences of its actions, rather than from being told what to do by a human.

7. Unsupervised Learning

Common techniques include clustering (grouping similar data points) and association (finding rules that describe large portions of the data).

8. Artificial Intelligence

AI involves creating algorithms that can perform tasks that typically require human intelligence, such as visual perception, speech recognition, decision-making, and language translation.

9. Machine Learning

ML algorithms build a model based on sample data, known as training data, to make predictions or decisions without being explicitly programmed to perform the task.

10. Deep Learning

Deep learning algorithms are particularly good at recognizing patterns in large amounts of unstructured data, such as images and speech.

11. Neural Networks

Neural networks consist of layers of nodes, or neurons, where each node is connected to others in a way that mimics how neurons in the brain connect.

12. Natural Language Processing

NLP is used in applications like speech recognition, language translation, sentiment analysis, and chatbots.

13. Computer Vision

This technology is used in applications like image and video recognition, self-driving cars, and facial recognition systems.

14. Supervised Learning

The algorithm learns to map the input to the output by comparing its predictions to the actual outcomes during training.

15. Algorithm

Algorithms are the backbone of AI, allowing computers to process data and learn from it.

16. Big Data

Big data provides the raw material that AI algorithms use to learn and make decisions.

17. Artificial Narrow Intelligence

Most AI systems in use today are examples of ANI, such as a recommendation system on a website or a virtual assistant.

18. Artificial Super Intelligence

ASI is a theoretical concept where AI not only performs any task a human can but does so at a superior level.
