The Dawn of AI

1941-1956

AI's roots began with Alan Turing's 1950 paper and the 1956 Dartmouth Conference, marking the official birth of AI.

The Early Years and Challenges

1956-1974

Early AI programs like the Logic Theorist showed potential, but limited computing power led to slow progress and the first "AI winter."

Knowledge-Based Systems and Expert Systems

1974-1980

Revival of AI through expert systems like MYCIN demonstrated success in specific domains, increasing commercial interest.

Expansion and Commercialization

1980-1990

Expert systems' commercial success led to increased investment, but their limitations caused another AI winter by the late 1980s.

Machine Learning and the Internet Age

1990-2000

Machine learning and neural networks gained prominence, and IBM's Deep Blue defeated Garry Kasparov, showcasing AI's potential.

Big Data and the AI Renaissance

2000-2010

Big data and deep learning revolutionized AI, with breakthroughs in image and speech recognition, exemplified by IBM's Watson.

AI in the Modern Era

2010-2020

AI became integral in daily life, with advancements in autonomous vehicles, virtual assistants, and ethical considerations rising.

Accelerated Advancements and Integration

2020-Present

AI's role in healthcare surged during COVID-19, with breakthroughs in quantum computing and increasing focus on AI ethics and regulation.
